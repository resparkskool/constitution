The Mission

We help ambitious people remember who they are so they can build lives, relationships, and businesses that actually reflect it.

This isn't self-improvement. It's self-remembrance.

You don't need to become someone new. You need to remember who you were before the world told you who to be — and Respark is where that fire gets lit again.

Who We're Building This For

Not a demographic. A feeling.

People who've built success on the outside and quietly checked out on the inside. Entrepreneurs, executives, business couples, parents — doesn't matter the job title or the relationship status. What matters is the sentence running underneath all of it:

"I've achieved a lot, but I don't feel like myself anymore."

That's who we're talking to. Every time.

The Enemy

Not failure. Not even burnout.

Disconnection.

From yourself. Your emotions. Your spouse or partner. Your purpose. Your values. Your own vision for your one life.

Respark exists to put that fire back out — by putting a different one on.

The Transformation

Before: Surviving a life that looks good and feels empty. Reactive, compartmentalized, performing a version of yourself for an audience that never asked for the performance.

After: Living from remembered identity. Grounded, honestly connected, integrated — business and marriage and self all burning from the same source instead of three separate matches.

We help ambitious people remember who they are and build lives they don't have to recover from.

The Five Embers We Tend

the pain points we address — and what catches fire on the other side
  1. Identity — "I don't know who I am anymore." → They remember who they are and build from it.
  2. Emotional Intelligence — Burnout, reactivity, anxiety, overwhelm. → They become emotionally resilient. Respond instead of react.
  3. Limiting Beliefs & Patterns — Self-sabotage, people-pleasing, never-enough. → They rewrite the unconscious operating system and choose on purpose.
  4. Relationships & Communication — Distance, conflict avoidance, broken trust. → They build honest, emotionally safe connection.
  5. Integration — Success at the expense of everything else. → Business, family, purpose, health, identity — one fire, not five.

The Respark Framework

  • R — Remember Who You Are (identity, values, self-awareness)
  • E — Examine Your Patterns (conditioning, trauma responses, beliefs)
  • S — Stabilize Your Emotions (nervous system, resilience, boundaries)
  • P — Pursue Honest Connection (communication, conflict, leadership)
  • A — Align Your Life (purpose, career, health, money, faith)
  • R — Rebuild Intentionally (habits, systems, environment)
  • K — Keep the Fire Burning (integration, community, legacy)

The Standard → Premium price raise ($7 → ~$47) fires when ALL three are true — not on a calendar:

  • 25–30 paid Standard members, and
  • 10–15+ showing real engagement (challenge completion, Q&A attendance, posting), and
  • 3–5 pieces of unsolicited positive feedback

Engagement is the actual signal. Signups alone don't light this match.

Premium bandwidth: needs a group-size cap decided before ads start — not yet finalized. Don't let this be the thing solved under pressure.

VIP activation: stays dark until Premium is stable at scale for ~60 days. Triggered by observable stability, not revenue panic.

Long-range Standard ladder: $7 → $47 → eventually $97/$125, each step triggered by the engagement criteria above — never just a date on a calendar.

2026 Projections — Scaffolding, Not Prophecy

Assumptions: modest cold-traffic conversion typical for a $7 low-ticket + quiz lead magnet, ads starting in 1–2 months, no major virality. Ranges to plan bandwidth around, not a forecast carved in stone.

Conservative — first 6 months of ads

  • 40–80 Standard signups → 15–25% engagement → 10–20 actually active
  • 10–20% upgrade to Premium → 8–16 Premium members
  • ~$400–900/mo combined — proof-of-concept, not income-replacing yet

Moderate — by year-end, assuming the price-raise trigger fires ~month 3–4

  • 150–300 cumulative Standard signups (mix of $7/$47 pricing)
  • Premium grows to 25–50 members if upgrade rate holds
  • ~$2,500–5,000/mo by year-end — Premium cap decision stops being theoretical here
  • VIP activation becomes a live conversation around this point, not before

Strong — engagement and word-of-mouth compounding (the 4/1 and 2/4 network advantage doing real work)

  • Premium could realistically push toward 60–100 by year-end
  • This is where the un-finalized Premium cap becomes the actual bottleneck on time — decide the number now, while it's calm
The ladder itself is sound. What decides the scenario is ad performance and engagement rate — neither knowable before launch. The job between now and launch isn't more pricing tweaks. It's locking the Premium cap and the call-ownership split.

Brand Voice

  • Confident and grounded. No hedging, no fluffy self-help language.
  • Warm and human — never corporate, never clinical.
  • Direct enough to name the pattern out loud; grounded enough that it feels safe, not gimmicky.
  • One resonant metaphor per piece, not five. The fire imagery is seasoning — not the whole meal.
  • Cold-traffic copy gestures at "ancient systems," "your unique wiring," "decoding who you actually are" — it does not name Human Design, astrology, or Gene Keys until someone's already inside the funnel.

What We Never Say

  • "Unlock your potential," "level up your life," or other coach-speak wallpaper (unless we're deliberately subverting it)
  • "Just manifest it," "good vibes only," or anything toxic-positive
  • Human Design / astrology / Gene Keys terminology in top-of-funnel, cold-traffic copy
  • Pricing in the quiz CTA or other evergreen assets — keeps it from going stale when offers change
  • Anything implying this is about becoming someone new. The language is always remembering, reconnecting, returning.

What We Always Believe

  • Transformation is remembrance, not reinvention.
  • Conflict and emotional intensity are curriculum, not failure — disconnection is the real enemy, not friction.
  • This work travels through relationship and trust — referral, network, reputation — not cold reach alone.
  • One clear message repeated consistently beats a new rebrand every few months.
  • Business and marriage aren't separate tracks. Integration is the whole point.
